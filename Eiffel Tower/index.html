Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, built in 1889 for the Paris Exposition Universelle, was designed by Gustave Eiffel. Initially criticized by many, it quickly became a global icon of French ingenuity and engineering. Standing 324 meters tall, it was the tallest man-made structure until 1930, drawing millions of visitors yearly as one of the world's most recognizable landmarks.

Eiffel Tower

Constructed using 18,038 iron parts and 2.5 million rivets, the tower showcases the architectural brilliance of its time. Despite initial plans to dismantle it after 20 years, its usefulness as a radio transmission tower ensured its survival. Today, it remains a symbol of France, admired for its elegant lattice structure and remarkable design.

The Eiffel Tower has three levels, each offering unique experiences. The first floor houses exhibitions and restaurants, while the second provides panoramic views of Paris. The third floor, at the top, offers breathtaking vistas, making it a favorite for tourists and romantics alike. It continues to be a must-see attraction, symbolizing Paris's enduring charm.
