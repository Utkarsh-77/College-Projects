Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, located in Amritsar, India, is one of the most revered Sikh shrines. Officially known as Harmandir Sahib, it was completed in 1604 and is renowned for its stunning gold-plated architecture. Surrounded by a serene pool of water, the temple reflects a blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles, symbolizing unity and inclusivity.

Inside, the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, is recited continuously. The temple attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists annually, offering free meals through its langar service, reflecting the Sikh values of community service and equality.

Visitors to the Golden Temple can experience its peaceful atmosphere and rich cultural heritage. The complex includes a museum and several smaller shrines. The surrounding Parikrama (walkway) and the Amrit Sarovar (holy tank) add to the spiritual ambiance. The temple's open arms welcome people from all walks of life, making it a symbol of harmony and devotion.
