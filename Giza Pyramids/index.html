Giza Pyramids

The Giza Pyramids, located on the Giza Plateau near Cairo, Egypt, are ancient wonders of the world. Built around 2580-2560 BC, they include the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure. These colossal structures served as elaborate tombs for Egyptian pharaohs and showcase remarkable architectural and engineering skills of ancient Egypt.

Giza Pyramids

The Great Pyramid, also known as the Pyramid of Khufu, is the largest and most famous. Originally standing at 146.6 meters, it was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years. The pyramids were constructed with precise alignment to the stars and the cardinal points, reflecting the ancient Egyptians' advanced understanding of astronomy and mathematics.

Today, the Giza Pyramids are a major tourist attraction and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors marvel at their grandeur and explore the surrounding complex, which includes the Sphinx and several smaller pyramids. The site continues to be a source of fascination and study, revealing insights into ancient Egyptian civilization and its enduring legacy.
