Bali Gates

Bali's iconic gates, particularly the Lempuyang Temple's "Gates of Heaven," have become a symbol of the island's spiritual allure. Located in East Bali, this temple complex offers breathtaking views of Mount Agung, Bali's highest volcano, framed perfectly by the temple's famous split gate. The temple itself is one of the oldest and most revered in Bali, dating back to the 14th century. Visitors often line up for photos at the gate, using a mirror trick to create the illusion of a reflection, as there's no actual water in front of the gate.

Bali Gates

Aside from its photogenic appeal, Lempuyang Temple is a significant religious site, so visitors must dress modestly and respect local customs. The best time to visit is early in the morning to avoid crowds, particularly during the dry season when the views are clearer.

Day Opening Hours Notes
Daily 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM The temple is open every day of the week.
Peak Season July-August, December Expect larger crowds during these months.
Off-Peak Season February-June, September-November Less crowded, ideal for visiting.
Best Time to Visit Early Morning (before 9:00 AM) Recommended to avoid long wait times for photos and to enjoy clearer views.
Cultural Events Varies (e.g., Piodalan every 210 days) Special ceremonies may occur, making it more crowded but also culturally enriching.
