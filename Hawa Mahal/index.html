Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is one of Jaipur's most iconic landmarks. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this five-story structure is a fine example of Rajput architecture with a unique blend of red and pink sandstone. The palace was designed by Lal Chand Ustad and constructed in the form of Lord Krishna's crown, reflecting the Maharaja's devotion.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is famous for its intricate fa√ßade, which features 953 small windows, or "jharokhas." These windows allowed royal women, who observed strict "purdah" (veiling), to watch street festivals and daily life without being seen by the public. The palace's name, meaning "Palace of Winds," comes from the cool breeze that flows through the jharokhas, keeping the interiors cool, even during Rajasthan's hot summers.

Despite its grandeur, Hawa Mahal has no foundation and is delicately balanced, making it one of the world's tallest structures of its kind. Inside, the palace has a series of chambers, courtyards, and passages leading to the upper floors, where visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Jaipur city.
