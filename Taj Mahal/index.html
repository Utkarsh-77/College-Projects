Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, built between 1632 and 1653 by Emperor Shah Jahan, is a symbol of love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Located in Agra, India, this white marble mausoleum was constructed in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Its intricate design features Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles, making it one of the most visited and admired structures worldwide.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal's main structure consists of a central dome surrounded by four minarets. The complex also includes gardens, a mosque, and a guest house. The monument's white marble changes color with the sunlight, creating a mesmerizing effect throughout the day. This architectural marvel is renowned for its symmetrical design and detailed craftsmanship, showcasing the artistic brilliance of the Mughal era.

In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural significance and universal appeal. Millions of visitors from around the world flock to see this "jewel of Muslim art" each year. Despite the passage of time and environmental challenges, the Taj Mahal continues to stand as a testament to eternal love and India's rich history.
